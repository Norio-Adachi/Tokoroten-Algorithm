# The "Tokoroten" Algorithm: Transparent Computation for Transcendental Numbers

### Philosophy: A Challenge to the World
Many calculation projects compete to extend the decimal digits of transcendental numbers, consuming immense energy and computational resources to build "black boxes" of unfathomable scale. 

**Our mission lies elsewhere.**

We do not merely "yield" the quest for 500 trillion digits to other challengers. Instead, we **invite you to challenge it.** As you push the boundaries of record-breaking, you will eventually encounter the inherent limitations of conventional algorithms: the lack of transparency, the inability to verify intermediate results, and the rigid requirement for pre-calculating values like √2.

When you find yourself lost in that desert, I offer you this "Tokoroten" algorithm—a transparent corridor where every step is not just a calculation, but a verified truth. We are not interested in the quantity of digits; we are dedicated to the quality of the process.

### The Path to Transparency
This project began with an attempt to revive the classic Gauss-Legendre algorithm. However, through the rigorous process of re-architecting it, I confronted a fundamental logical barrier: the necessity of the initial square root calculation. I realized that this "pre-calculation" was a shackle upon the elegance of true mathematics. 

True computation should not be the carving of a giant, static ice block. It should be the fluid extraction of truth—like Tokoroten—where every slice is transparent, verifiable, and meaningful in real-time.
